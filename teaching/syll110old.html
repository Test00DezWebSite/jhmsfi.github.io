73/88-110 Social Decision Making, Fall 1999

(aka Experiments with Economic Principles)

    Goal: A major part of social science is devoted to the study of how individuals and societies make choices. The purpose of this course is for you to gain a deep understanding of this topic.

    This course differs from a conventional social science course in that we present all of the relevant material through the use of in-class experiments rather than lectures. It is our hope that the use of experiments will make the class both enjoyable and insightful. The experiments will allow you to directly participate in the abstract ideas discussed in the class (this tends to make learning much easier). Given the difficulty of reproducing the experiments on your own, make sure you always attend and participate during these sessions.

    After the experiment you will need to try and organize your thoughts about what occurred and discover the underlying principles of the system. This is not an easy thing to do, but your skill will improve with practice.

    We hope that you will leave the class with both a deep understanding of what we feel are some of the most important principles underlying social decisions and a general set of skills that will allow you to discover new ideas on your own.
