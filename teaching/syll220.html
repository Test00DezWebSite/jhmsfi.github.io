  • Materials:

    1. [EEP] Theodore Bergstrom and John H. Miller, Experiments with Economic Principles, McGraw-Hill, 1997. You should also keep track of the errata for this book. (Available at the fine CMU book store.)

    2. (Strongly Recommended) Robert H. Frank, Microeconomics and Behavior 2nd or 3rd Edition, McGraw-Hill, 1994. (Also available at the CMU book store. In the syllabus, readings for the second edition are designated [MB2]. For the third edition look under [MB3].)

    3. On occasion, you may need to download some files.

    4. An important part of the class is connecting the material we learn with the real world. For each chapter of Experiments with Economic Principles, we have a collection of relevant newspaper articles, make sure you read them as we work through the material. You may also want to keep track of the Time daily news summary, The Economist, or similar services for news about real policy making. You can also search the web for general public policy sites or look at the site maintained at CMU. Here is a link to the Iowa Political Stock Market. If you're interested, you can get information on all of the Nobel prize winners in economics (a particularly interesting story is the one about the "Lost Years" of John Nash). Of course, you also may want to know a few economist jokes.
