The Scobey Prize

The Scobey Prize is awarded to members of the SDS community to highlight notable achievements.

The Scobey Story

F.E. Scobey served as Director of the U.S. Mint from March, 1922, to September, 1923. He and Warren Harding enjoyed fishing, poker playing, and golfing together, and Scobey's loyal friendship (and campaign contributions) resulted in his appointment as Director under Harding's administration, despite any apparent qualifications. His brief stint at the Mint is notable for the fact that "he did little damage."

Recipients:

  • Peter Thompson, December, 2002. For actually reading all of the graduate student and job market seminar papers prior to the seminars.
  • Steven Klepper, April, 2002. For proving that even one-handed economists can have two opinions (and for that Schumpeter thing).
  • Roberto Weber, January, 2002. For teaching excellence in Fall, 2001 (no really, we're serious).
