Modelling Applause and Ovation

Jorge Galindo - Harvard University
Jose Penalva Zuasti - UCLA
Yuri Yegorov - University Pompeu Fabra
Eduardo Zambrano - Cornell University

Abstract:

We formulate a framework for modelling the process of applause and eventual ovation that takes place after a certain event has concluded. The model allows for heterogeneity regarding the effect that the quality of the event and the behavior of others have in the actions of each individual, for differential access to information based on spacial location, and for the fact that individuals will feel less reward from clapping and standing after having done so for a certain amount of time, which actually depends on the behavior of others. Within this framework, and without any assumption requesting that the crucial type of people be seated at places that are more conducive to 'seeding' the process of clapping and ovation, we generate fairly robust and rugged ovations under a wide variety of the parameters that define the landscape of preferences. The results encourage us to find applications of these type of models to processes of technological diffusion and obsolesence.
