Risking Reputations: when to endorse new drugs?

  • We explore the strategies doctors might adopt to manage their reputation when faced with the choice of publicly adopting a new prescription drug. In particular we ask whether doctors should choose to be forerunners on taking a public stance early on in the drug experimentation process, risking their reputation at first, but with the prospect of eventually gaining much credit for having been perceptive before the crowd. We thus explore the waiting times until drug adoption that doctors learn, and whether these waiting times come to depend on reputation and on the riskiness of the drug.
