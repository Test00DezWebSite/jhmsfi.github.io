No clear dominated strategy
•In the E=150 case; 76 out of the 81 possible strategy ends up in the winning pool. E=120 case; 68 out of 81 strategy in the winning pool.
•So, in a non-dynamic game, there is no clearly dominant or dominated strategy due to the large number of players.
•At later time-steps, however, agents that are clearly behind have no reason to slow down.
•In the second case, when resources are limited, it is advantageous to be slightly aggressive while following and less aggressive while leading.
